Lesson: Ocean Wave Energy and Calculation Examples

Ocean wave energy, also known as wave power, captures the energy produced by surface waves to generate electricity. It leverages both the kinetic and potential energy of waves. As wind moves over the ocean surface, it transfers energy to the water, creating waves that can be harnessed for power generation.

Formula for Wave Energy per Unit Length

Wave Energy per Unit Length:

\[ E = \frac{1}{8} \rho g H^2 \]

Where:
\( E \) = Energy per unit length (Joules/meter)
\( \rho \) = Water density (approx. 1025 kg/mÂ³ for seawater)
\( g \) = Acceleration due to gravity (9.81 m/sÂ²)
\( H \) = Wave height (in meters)

Example 1: Wave Energy per Unit Length Calculation

Given:

Wave height (\( H \)) = 3 meters
Water density (\( \rho \)) = 1025 kg/mÂ³
Gravity (\( g \)) = 9.81 m/sÂ²

Plug these values into the formula:
\[ E = \frac{1}{8} \times 1025 \times 9.81 \times (3)^2 = 11,318.46 \, \text{J/m} \]

Result: 11,318.46 Joules/meter

Formula for Wave Power per Unit Length

Wave Power per Unit Length:

\[ P = \left( \frac{\rho g^2}{64\pi} \right) H_{m0}^2 T_e \]

Where:
\( P \) = Wave power per unit length (Watts/meter)
\( \rho \) = Water density (1025 kg/mÂ³)
\( g \) = Acceleration due to gravity (9.81 m/sÂ²)
\( H_{m0} \) = Significant wave height (in meters)
\( T_e \) = Energy period (in seconds)

Example 2: Wave Power per Unit Length Calculation

Given:

Significant wave height (\( H_{m0} \)) = 4 meters
Energy period (\( T_e \)) = 10 seconds
Water density (\( \rho \)) = 1025 kg/mÂ³
Gravity (\( g \)) = 9.81 m/sÂ²

Plug these values into the formula:
\[ P = \left( \frac{1025 \, \text{kg/m}^3 \times (9.81 \, \text{m/s}^2)^2}{64 \, \pi} \right) \times (4 \, \text{m})^2 \times 10 \, \text{s} \]

Calculating further:
\[ P = \left( \frac{1025 \times 96.2361}{64 \, \pi} \right) \times 16 \times 10 = \left( \frac{98641.7}{201.06} \right) \times 160 = 490.56 \times 160 = 78489.6 \, \text{W/m} \]

Result: 78,489.6 Watts/meter

Simplified Wave Power per Unit Length Formula

Wave Power per Unit Length (Approximation):

\[ P \approx 0.5 \times H_{m0}^2 \times T_3 \]

Where:
\( P \) = Wave power per unit length (kW/m)
\( H_{m0} \) = Significant wave height (in meters)
\( T_3 \) = Mean wave period of the highest one-third of waves (in seconds)

Example 3: Simplified Wave Power Calculation

Given:

Significant wave height (\( H_{m0} \)) = 3 meters
Mean wave period (\( T_3 \)) = 8 seconds

Plug these values into the formula:
\[ P \approx 0.5 \times (3)^2 \times 8 = 36 \, \text{kW/m} \, (36,000 \, \text{W/m}) \]

Result: 36 kW/m (36,000 Watts/meter)
